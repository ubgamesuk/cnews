Exciting new redevelopment plans revealed for Manor Walks Shopping Centre in plan to increase value to £100 million


6TH APR 2026

CramlingtonNews Editors

Exciting new redevelopment plans have been revealed by the owners of the Manor Walks Shopping Centre, Advance Northumberland, to create major improvements to the centre - alongside the construction of a brand new retail unit on the former Police Station site on Westmorland Way.

This comes as part of Advance Northumberland's ambitious plan to increase the value of the Cramlington centre to £100 million by 2029 - where they will sell it to a new buyer at the end of the decade. The plans pictured on this post were shown at a Northumberland County Council meeting last week, where Advance Northumberland announced their plans for this major development to increase their sale value.

ClIr Mark Swinburn, Councillor for Cramlington Village said: "We have been working with Advance, the Town Council, the County regeneration team, and Highways to work on improvements to the town centre as well as traffic and congestion improvements with surveys completed and plans being worked up for changes to improve traffic flow."

"It has been almost uniformly agreed with councillors that Manor Walks should not have been bought with public loans, and this was the wrong decision, as we said at the time. No scrutiny was carried out at that time and it was forced through. The result being that it lost value very quickly! Work is now ongoing to increase value, and the surrounding land with commercial investment, to make this a more viable asset."

Work should be complete by 2029, where Advance are anticipating to make a sale.
